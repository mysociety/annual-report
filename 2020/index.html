Digital democracy goes global

With the Westminster Foundation for Democracy and the United Nations Development Programme, we‚Äôve worked with parliaments in Myanmar, Morocco, Armenia, Bhutan, Pakistan and Timor-Leste as they undergo the process of digitalisation.

Putting parliamentary services online has never been more urgent ‚Äî and, aptly enough, most of the work had to be conducted remotely.

/*[Bec/Zarino photos]*/

As a designer, it was fascinating seeing how another country‚Äôs parliament works, and the challenges faced by MPs and their support staff, in representing a citizenry with completely different economic and technological challenges to anywhere else I‚Äôd seen.

Work we did with the Moroccan parliament demonstrated that citizens there preferred some of the changes to parliamentary activity made as a result of COVID-19, especially the move to broadcast parliamentary sessions on TV.

This has enabled citizens to inform themselves and better hold their parliamentarians to account, and we have recommended that these broadcasts are continued beyond the pandemic.

What we did in 2020

We started 2020 with a commitment to the climate.

As that includes, of course, a look at our own activities, we formed the mySociety Climate Action Group, open for any staff member to join. Together, they create new policies and assess the improvements we can make.

Some of the things we‚Äôve done so far (find more detail in our environment policy):

  • Made a decision to offset not just our travel, but all our carbon emissions
  • Investigated the worth of offset schemes, deciding to make our own offsets directly
  • Restricted business travel (once that sort of thing is permitted again, that is)
  • Joined Climate Perks to incentivise sustainable holiday travel
  • Extended the life of our hardware
  • Assessed third party suppliers on their climate commitments
  • Calculated our existing carbon footprint, so we‚Äôll know how much progress we‚Äôre making
  • Committed to making the climate a primary consideration when renewing our hosting contracts

‚Ä¶and there‚Äôs plenty more to come next year.

Who we worked with‚Ä¶

In January, we were able to start making good on our commitment to help those fighting climate change, providing the website for the UK‚Äôs national Climate Assembly.

TICTeC 2020 ¬∑ Virtual event ¬∑ 24th‚Äì25th March

We‚Äôd planned our annual Impacts of Civic Tech conference as usual: we‚Äôd have been convening in Reykjavik, reconnecting with friends old and new from around the world.

In March. And we all know what happened to March.

And so, it was a quick turnaround time: as with so many in-person events, large and small, we had to take it online (in just ten days!). Nanjala Nyabola‚Äôs keynote session felt more intimate, even as she was giving us a sweeping global perspective on how the internet era is transforming politics.

{% include image.html src="https://place-hold.it/540x350" width="540" height="350" alt="" loading="lazy" class="card-img-top" %}

And our fireside chat with our other keynote speaker, Dr. Hollie Russon Gilman, highlighted that the pandemic is an important opportunity to reimagine how civic tools can be used in a way to build civic power.

Other notable TICTeC sessions included Civic Hall presenting their latest research on how organisations have managed to survive, and even thrive, in the difficult field of civic engagement, social change, and the digital transformation of democracy.

Neema Iyer from Pollicy presented their large-scale research study on the prevalence of online-based violence against women across five countries in Africa, and how this affects their access to civic technology.

Sruti Modekurty from OpenAQ talked about how their work making access to air quality data openly available has had a real impact on influencing policy, and ultimately improved the health of citizens.

These examples, and the others we heard at TICTeC 2020, are just the kind of evidence-based impact and research stories we love to hear at TICTeC events, to inform the future development of civic technology.
